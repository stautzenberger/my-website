On the Futility of Debate

Posted on 2022-06-29 • 357 words • 2 minute read

Idealism aside, in general we debate not to change our ideas but to reinforce our convictions. The most sensible thing, in this sense, is to assume that the opponent is not someone who is interested in adopting one’s ideas. Whoever enters the battlefield wanting to fall before the other is because he is either suicidal or an imbecile. There is something noble, our democratic culture tells us, in the exchange of ideas and dialogue between those who think differently. It may be true for many, but clearly not for me. Debating, especially on the internet, consumes time and yields very few benefits.

There is the possibility of disenchantment of one’s own ideas. If that is the case, there are better ways to reassess ideologies: books or this blog, for example. Let’s think about a valuable concept that economic science has left us: the division of labor. Different people fulfill different roles in society. Speaking in these terms, there are those who provide information and those who receive it. It should not surprise anyone that, for some time now, I have dedicated myself solely to writing essays that I do not need to defend (since mere argumentation should suffice) when I want to be serious about certain topics. There is a reason why some people are dedicated only to developing and transmitting information. It is a talent that not everyone has or can acquire. The fact that two people share the same ideas does not make them equal. One will have a better pen to put them down. If this were not so, then everyone would be excellent writers. But that’s not how human nature works: we were never a species destined for equality of conditions or results. That is why we reactionaries recognize hierarchies, with all the narcissism that this entails.

It is not difficult to anticipate the opponent’s arguments. But no matter what the truth is, the tulip won’t change its mind, and I won’t waste time trying to convince it that it’s wrong. There are more productive things to do, and the truth is that all that books is not going to be seen alone.
